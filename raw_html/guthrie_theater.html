SEASON TICKETS

Made to move you

Our upcoming 2026–2027 Season covers vast theatrical ground: From the existential dialogue in Waiting for Godot to the silly-yet-soaring music in The Pirates of Penzance, each story explores the realities of what it means to be human — sometimes through laughter or absurdity; other times through sorrow or suspense. 

When we choose to experience theater together, something remarkable happens: Hope breaks through and propels us forward. This season is made to move you in big and small ways, and we can’t wait to experience it together.

Introducing digital tickets

To make ticket delivery more secure and convenient, we’ve partnered with True Tickets, a leader in digital ticketing. Digital tickets allow you to access and share your Guthrie tickets at tickets.guthrietheater.org from any smart mobile device — no paper, no hassle.

You don’t need an app, and you can save your tickets to Apple or Google Wallet. Just select digital tickets at checkout to get started.

Opening soon: Indígena by Owamni

Last October, we were thrilled to announce that Owamni was moving to the Guthrie’s restaurant space.

To mark this exciting new chapter, the James Beard Award-winning restaurant founded by Chef Sean Sherman recently announced it will evolve its name to Indígena by Owamni (pronounced: in-DEE-heh-nah). In addition to expanding its culinary scope and showcasing a broader diversity of Indigenous cuisines, Indígena by Owamni will introduce a pre-theater menu tailored for Guthrie patrons, plus offer post-show drinks and bar food service.

The restaurant is slated to open at the Guthrie on June 23, and reservations are available through OpenTable.
